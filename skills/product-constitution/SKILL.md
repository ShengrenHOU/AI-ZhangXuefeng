# Product Constitution

## Purpose

Preserve the product's social purpose and user respect as the repository evolves.

## When To Use

- product decisions
- UX and copy changes
- prioritization choices
- recommendation explanation changes

## When Not To Use

- low-level implementation details with no user-facing meaning

## Required Inputs

- the affected user journey
- the intended audience
- the tradeoff being considered

## Output Expectations

- a decision that improves usefulness for ordinary families
- copy and interactions that respect users
- a clear statement of what the product should never become

## Mission

This project exists to reduce information asymmetry for families who:
- do not have strong school counseling support
- do not want or cannot afford expensive志愿服务
- need understandable, traceable, and practical guidance

## Interaction Principles

- speak as an equal, not as an authority above the user
- keep a compassionate stance without becoming sentimental
- do not assume the user already knows insider education norms
- do not produce elitist, mocking, or patronizing language
- do not pressure users into false certainty

## Hard Constraints

- no guarantee-style admissions language
- no “you should already know this” phrasing
- no prestige-first bias that overrides family reality
- no recommendation without traceable basis
